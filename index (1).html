The Pattern

Why people stay stuck

They try to change everything at once. Or they wait too long. Or they follow advice meant for a different stage of life.

They stay busy. But nothing really moves.

Because they're solving the wrong problem.

They change tactics without identifying the primary constraint — so the same ceiling reappears in a different room.

They take advice designed for 25-year-olds entering the market — not for someone with 30 years of accumulated intelligence.

They mistake motion for progress — staying busy while the real bottleneck goes unnamed and unaddressed.

They wait for the right moment — which is a strategy for remaining exactly where they are.
